Biden, Harris watch inaugural prayer service remotely from White House

By John Wagner

Biden and Harris, along with their spouses and other family members, watched from the White House on Thursday a virtual version of the traditional inaugural prayer service held by Washington National Cathedral — another change brought about by the coronavirus pandemic.

Several rows of socially distanced chairs were set up in the State Dining Room, with multiple screens showing a stream of an interfaith service that included remarks from a who’s who of faith leaders associated with liberal causes.

During the three minutes that a press pool was permitted to observe, everyone in the room stood as Patti LaBelle sang the national anthem.

Many of the speakers offered prayers for the new president and his administration. The Rev. William J. Barber II, a North Carolina preacher and anti-poverty crusader, was scheduled to deliver the homily.
